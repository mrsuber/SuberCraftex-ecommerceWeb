# SuberCraftex Master Curriculum System
## The Complete Path from Beginner to Spacecraft Builder

**Vision**: Every apprentice who completes these curricula can build a complete rocket from scratch - from cutting wood and casting metal, to programming flight computers and integrating avionics.

---

## 📚 Complete Curriculum Overview

### 1. **Woodworking → Aerospace Manufacturing** (12 levels, 200+ assignments)
**The MASTER curriculum that connects everything**

- **Level 1 (Months 1-3)**: Workshop Safety & Hand Tools
  - Safety certification, hand tool mastery, sharpening, stock preparation
  - Power tool fundamentals: table saw, jointer, planer
  - Wood science: species, moisture content, wood movement

- **Level 2 (Months 4-6)**: Joinery Fundamentals
  - Mortise & tenon, dovetails, box joints, finger joints
  - Furniture construction principles
  - Advanced joinery: bridle joints, through-dovetails, sliding dovetails

- **Level 3 (Months 7-9)**: Fusion 360 Introduction & Furniture Design
  - Fusion 360 fundamentals for woodworking
  - Parametric design, assemblies, 2D drawings
  - CAM basics for CNC routing
  - **Cross-ref**: None yet - introduces digital design

- **Level 4 (Months 10-12)**: Advanced Furniture & CNC
  - Complex furniture: tables, chairs, cabinets
  - CNC router operation and programming
  - Veneer work, inlay, marquetry
  - **Cross-ref Tailoring (Level 3-4)**: Upholstery for chair seats and cushions
  - **Cross-ref Embroidery (Level 1-2)**: Decorative stitching for upholstered furniture

- **Level 5 (Months 13-15)**: Wood Bending, Lamination & Composites
  - Steam bending, bent lamination, vacuum bag press
  - Introduction to composite materials (fiberglass, carbon fiber)
  - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 1)**: Python scripts for bend calculations

- **Level 6 (Months 16-18)**: Metalworking Foundations
  - Metal shop safety, material science, spark test
  - MIG welding, TIG welding (aluminum & steel)
  - Sheet metal: brake work, rolling, forming
  - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 1)**: Safety practices transfer

- **Level 7 (Months 19-21)**: Advanced Welding & Fabrication
  - Stick welding, pipe welding
  - Tube notching, roll cage fabrication
  - Structural welding certification (AWS D1.1)
  - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 2-3)**: Weld Arduino-controlled welding fixtures

- **Level 8 (Months 22-24)**: Precision Machining
  - Manual lathe: turning, threading, tapers
  - Manual mill: slots, pockets, holes, dividing head
  - Fusion 360 CAM for machining
  - Precision measurement: micrometers, indicators, gauge blocks
  - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 2)**: CNC G-code programming

- **Level 9 (Months 25-27)**: CNC Machining & Advanced CAM
  - CNC lathe and mill operation
  - Advanced Fusion 360 CAM: 3+2 axis, multi-axis
  - Fixturing, work holding, tool selection
  - Production machining techniques

- **Level 10 (Months 28-30)**: Foundry & Casting
  - Foundry safety, furnace construction
  - Aluminum sand casting, pattern making
  - **Iron casting** - melt and pour iron for structural parts
  - Investment casting, lost-wax process
  - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 5)**: Temperature controllers for furnaces

- **Level 11 (Months 31-33)**: Aerospace Materials & Standards
  - Aerospace aluminum alloys (6061, 7075, 2024)
  - Titanium, Inconel, stainless steel
  - AS9100 quality standards, traceability
  - NDT: X-ray, ultrasonic, dye penetrant
  - Heat treating, stress relieving

- **Level 12 (Months 34-42)**: Complete Rocket Fabrication
  - **Assignment WA-12.1.1**: Rocket motor case (pressure vessel design, FEA, machining)
  - **Assignment WA-12.1.2**: Airframe fabrication (filament winding or machined aluminum)
  - **Assignment WA-12.1.3**: Fin attachment and alignment (±0.5° tolerance)
  - **Assignment WA-12.1.4**: Nose cone (aerodynamic shaping, surface finish)
  - **Assignment WA-12.1.5**: Recovery bay (parachute compartment, electronics bay)
  - **Assignment WA-12.2.1**: Avionics integration bay
    - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 12)**: Install flight computer, sensors, batteries
    - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 11)**: Flash flight software
  - **Assignment WA-12.2.2**: Parachute system
    - **Cross-ref Tailoring (Level 5-6)**: Sew parachutes to spec
    - **Cross-ref Embroidery (Level 6)**: Mission patch embroidery
  - **Assignment WA-12.3.1**: Ground support equipment
    - Launch rail, blast deflector, igniter clips
  - **Assignment WA-12.3.2**: Furniture for mission control
    - **Cross-ref Tailoring (Level 4)**: Operator chair upholstery
    - **Cross-ref Embroidery (Level 4)**: Logo embroidery on chairs
    - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 8)**: Console wiring and displays
    - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 11)**: Ground station software
  - **Assignment WA-12.4.1**: CAPSTONE - Complete rocket assembly, testing, and flight
    - Integration of ALL subsystems
    - Static fire test
    - Flight readiness review
    - Launch, flight, recovery
    - **Cross-ref ALL CURRICULA**: This is where EVERYTHING connects

### 2. **Electronics** (12 levels, ~180 assignments)
**The nervous system - sensors, circuits, avionics**

- **Level 1**: Safety, Ohm's Law, components, soldering, breadboarding
- **Level 2**: Diodes, transistors, op-amps
- **Level 3**: Digital logic, Arduino, sensors (temp, pressure, IMU)
  - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 1-2)**: Arduino programming
- **Level 4**: ESP32, WiFi/Bluetooth, IoT
  - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 5-7)**: Cloud connectivity
- **Level 5**: PCB design (KiCad), 2-layer boards, manufacturing
  - **Cross-ref Woodworking (Level 8+)**: Design custom PCBs for rocket
- **Level 6**: 4-layer PCBs, impedance control, high-speed design
- **Level 7**: Power electronics, battery management, motor drivers
- **Level 8**: RF design, antennas, telemetry systems
- **Level 9**: IMU, GPS, sensor fusion, navigation
  - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 10)**: Kalman filtering algorithms
- **Level 10**: Flight computer hardware design
- **Level 11**: Pyrotechnic circuits, igniter firing, safety interlocks
  - **Cross-ref Woodworking (Level 12)**: Motor ignition system
- **Level 12**: Complete avionics integration
  - **Cross-ref Woodworking (Level 12)**: Install in rocket
  - **Cross-ref Computing (Level 11)**: Flight software integration

### 3. **Computing** (12 levels, ~180 assignments)
**The brain - software, control systems, AI**

- **Level 1**: Command line, Python, algorithms
- **Level 2**: C programming, ARM Cortex bare-metal
  - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 3-4)**: Program microcontrollers
- **Level 3**: FreeRTOS, real-time systems
  - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 4)**: ESP32 multi-tasking
- **Level 4**: Communication protocols (UART, SPI, I2C, CAN, USB)
- **Level 5**: Sensor fusion, Kalman filtering
  - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 9)**: Process IMU data
- **Level 6**: PID control, state-space, feedback loops
- **Level 7**: Data logging, SD cards, flash memory
- **Level 8**: Ground station software, GUI, visualization
  - **Cross-ref Woodworking (Level 12)**: Mission control console
- **Level 9**: Computer vision (OpenCV), object detection
- **Level 10**: Flight software architecture, state machines
- **Level 11**: Rocket flight software (apogee, deployment, telemetry)
  - **Cross-ref Electronics (Level 11-12)**: Integrated with avionics
  - **Cross-ref Woodworking (Level 12)**: Complete rocket system
- **Level 12**: AI/ML for manufacturing, data centers
  - **Cross-ref ALL**: Optimize SuberCraftex operations

## 🔗 Critical Integration Points

### For Rocket Construction (Woodworking Level 12):

1. **Airframe Structure**
   - Woodworking: Machine aluminum airframe tubes
   - Welding: Weld motor mount and fin attachments
   - Fusion 360: Design all components with FEA

2. **Recovery System**
   - Tailoring: Sew parachutes (main & drogue)
   - Embroidery: Mission patch, parachute labels
   - Electronics: Deployment altimeter, pyro charges
   - Computing: Deployment software logic

3. **Avionics Bay**
   - Electronics: Design flight computer PCB
   - Computing: Write flight software
   - Woodworking: Machine electronics sled/mounting
   - 3D Printing: Custom connectors and brackets

4. **Propulsion**
   - Woodworking: Machine motor case (pressure vessel)
   - Foundry: Cast nozzle (graphite or phenolic)
   - Electronics: Igniter circuit, continuity testing
   - Computing: Ignition sequencing software

5. **Ground Systems**
   - Woodworking: Build launch rail, mission control furniture
   - Electronics: Build launch controller, telemetry receiver
   - Computing: Ground station software
   - Tailoring: Operator chair upholstery
   - Embroidery: Logo on chairs and uniforms

6. **Testing & Documentation**
   - ALL curricula: Comprehensive testing procedures
   - Printing Press: Labels, documentation, signage
   - Computing: Data logging and analysis

## 📈 Apprentice Progression Paths

### Path 1: Complete Rocket Builder (All Curricula)
**Duration**: 4-5 years
**Outcome**: Can build complete rocket from raw materials
- Start: Woodworking Level 1 (safety)
- Parallel: Electronics & Computing (starting Month 7)
- Integration: Tailoring & Embroidery (as needed for projects)
- Capstone: Complete rocket (Month 42+)

**Duration**: 3-3.5 years
**Outcome**: Design and build flight computers

**Outcome**: Master fabricator (wood & metal)
